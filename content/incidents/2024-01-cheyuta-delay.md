The Congress government's Cheyuta scheme — which promised ₹1 lakh to every woman on turning 18 as part of its Six Guarantees — had not been launched by April 20, 2024, well past the 100-day deadline of March 16, 2024 and into the fourth month of the government's tenure. Women & Child Development Minister Seethakka (Danasari Anasuya) cited database preparation challenges, stating that the beneficiary identification process involving Aadhaar-seeding and coordination with the Civil Registration System was still underway.

Opposition BRS leaders staged demonstrations at district collectorates across Hyderabad and Warangal, demanding the immediate launch of the scheme. They alleged the delay was financial rather than administrative, pointing to the state's fiscal difficulties after the Congress government had already launched several other guarantee schemes. The government denied fiscal constraints were the cause and indicated the scheme would launch in phases once verification was complete.

Civil society groups working with young women in rural Telangana noted that many 18-year-olds who had been counting on the lump sum for education or economic independence were left in uncertainty. No revised launch date was officially announced at the time of this incident. The scheme subsequently saw a partial rollout in late 2024, but statewide coverage remained incomplete through early 2026.
