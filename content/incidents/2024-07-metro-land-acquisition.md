Land acquisition proceedings for Hyderabad Metro Rail Phase 2 corridors — particularly the MGBS-Chandrayangutta alignment passing through densely built-up areas of old city Hyderabad — encountered significant resistance from property owners and commercial establishments by July 2024. Court injunctions filed by affected landowners in Rangareddy and Hyderabad districts paused the social impact assessment process on two stretches.

The Hyderabad Metro Rail Limited (HMRL) stated that the alignment had been designed to minimise displacement, but residents along the proposed Nagole-Meerpet extension argued that compensation rates were below market value and that affected households had not been given adequate notice. Several petitions were pending before the National Green Tribunal (NGT) over tree felling required for corridor construction.

With land acquisition disputes unresolved, ground-breaking on the most contentious packages remained on hold through mid-2024, putting the government's Year 1 tendering target under pressure.
