Reports from Mahbubnagar district and several other districts emerged in November 2024 indicating that approximately 8 lakh Aasara social security pension beneficiaries — including the elderly, disabled, and widows — had not received pension payments for up to six months. Beneficiaries in rural mandals of Mahbubnagar complained that their monthly ₹2,500 pension (already below the promised ₹4,000) had not been credited since May 2024.

Finance Minister Bhatti Vikramarka Mallu acknowledged payment delays in a press conference, attributing them to a technical transition in the beneficiary database following the exclusion of ineligible entries that had accumulated under the previous BRS government. He stated that pending payments would be cleared in a phased manner within the next 60 days, but did not specify which beneficiaries would receive arrears first.

Social welfare organisations in Mahbubnagar held demonstrations outside district collectorates, with elderly beneficiaries among the protesters. The situation was particularly acute for widows and disabled individuals for whom the monthly pension constituted their primary income. By November 2024, the government had also not implemented the promised ₹4,000/month enhancement — leaving beneficiaries doubly affected by both the arrears and the undelivered hike.
