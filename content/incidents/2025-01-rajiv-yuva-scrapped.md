By January 2025, it became evident that the Rajiv Yuva Vikasam scheme — which promised ₹4,000 per month to unemployed youth as one of the Congress party's Six Guarantees ahead of the 2023 elections — had been quietly removed from the government's active policy agenda. No Government Order had been issued, no budget line was allocated in the 2024-25 interim or full budget, and the scheme was absent from official implementation timelines published by the Finance Ministry.

Chief Minister A. Revanth Reddy, in a press interaction, pivoted to framing the government's approach as job creation rather than cash transfers, citing recruitment notifications for government posts and skill development initiatives. He did not directly address the ₹4,000/month cash allowance commitment or explain why it had not been implemented. Opposition BRS and BJP leaders termed this a "betrayal of youth" and pointed to the Six Guarantees manifesto language which had explicitly promised a monthly cash allowance.

Youth organisations affiliated with both opposition parties held protests at Dharna Chowk in Hyderabad, demanding either implementation of the scheme or an official formal announcement of its abandonment with stated reasons. As of this filing, no formal communication from the government acknowledging or withdrawing the scheme exists — the promise has effectively been dropped by omission rather than any official decision.
