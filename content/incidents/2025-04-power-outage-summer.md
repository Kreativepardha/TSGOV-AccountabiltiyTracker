Residents across rural mandals of Warangal, Nalgonda, Karimnagar, and Kurnool reported power outages lasting 8 to 12 hours per day during April–May 2025. This occurred despite the Congress government's election promise to eliminate power cuts and provide 24-hour supply to agricultural consumers.

TSSPDCL and TSNPDCL attributed the cuts to high peak demand during summer, transformer failures, and insufficient generation capacity. Consumer groups and opposition parties alleged the situation was worse than pre-election promises had suggested.

The government stated that it was working to procure additional power from central grid allocation and that the situation would normalise by June 2025.

> **Note:** This incident is linked to the [200-units-free-power](/promises/200-units-free-power) promise, which covers the free electricity scheme for domestic consumers.
