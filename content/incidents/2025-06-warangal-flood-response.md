Heavy rains in June 2025 caused severe flooding in Warangal, Mahabubabad, and Khammam districts, affecting hundreds of villages. Multiple news outlets reported that relief operations were delayed by 48–72 hours in several submerged areas due to road damage cutting off access.

The Revenue Department stated that NDRF teams were deployed within 24 hours and that 12,000 people were evacuated to relief camps. Journalists on the ground reported that some low-lying villages remained without relief for over three days.

The Chief Minister conducted an aerial survey and announced ex-gratia payments of ₹5 lakh per death and ₹10,000 immediate relief per affected family. Relief operations were ongoing as of the time of entry.
